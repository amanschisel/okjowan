1. What would you call the edges of a Cornish pasty ?

A.

There is no real name for it.
It is 'crimped', so you could call it a 'crimp', but that's not really it's name.
It used to be filled with jam, and you would eat it at the end.
Also, the minners used to leave it down the mines for the 'tinners' - the ghosts of the dead miners.
